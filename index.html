Each source publishes a different indicator. Anthropic measures relative concentration on Claude.ai. OpenAI ranks countries by messages-per-capita on consumer plans. Microsoft estimates population penetration via cross-product telemetry. OpenRouter tracks token volume by model on its developer API gateway. The four are not directly comparable — but together they cover both the consumer and developer sides of AI usage.

Anthropic
Economic Index — AUI
What it measures
Ratio of Claude.ai sample share to working-age population share. AUI > 1 = over-represented; < 1 = under-represented.
Data source
Sampled Claude.ai consumer conversations + API usage (Sept 2025 release added geographic cuts).
Coverage
150+ countries; all 50 US states + DC.
Strengths
Open-sourced underlying data; intersected with SOC task classifications; tracks augmentation vs. automation.
Caveats
Single-platform measure (Claude.ai only). Concentration metric, not penetration.
OpenAI
Signals — Messages per capita
What it measures
Country rank by messages sent per capita on consumer ChatGPT (Free, Go, Plus, Pro).
Data source
Privacy-preserving sample of messages July 2024 – March 2026, updated quarterly.
Coverage
Countries with population ≥ 5M. Excludes Belarus, China, Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Russia, Venezuela.
Strengths
Largest single-product dataset; topic and demographic decomposition; downloadable time series.
Caveats
Excludes Codex, Enterprise, Education → understates workplace use. Several major markets not covered.
Microsoft
AI Diffusion Report
What it measures
Share of working-age population (15–64) that has used a generative AI product in the period.
Data source
Aggregated, anonymized Microsoft telemetry, adjusted for OS/device share, internet penetration, country population.
Coverage
100+ markets, full population-normalized diffusion rates.
Strengths
Cross-product (captures any genAI use, not only Microsoft); true penetration metric; quarterly cadence.
Caveats
Telemetry skewed toward Windows/Edge users; adjustment model assumptions are not fully transparent.
OpenRouter
Rankings — Token volume
What it measures
Weekly tokens consumed by model through the OpenRouter API gateway. Captures the developer/API side of AI usage, not consumer adoption.
Data source
Aggregated production traffic from 5M+ developers and applications routing requests through OpenRouter. Weekly cadence.
Coverage
~20 trillion tokens/week processed; hundreds of models across all major frontier and open-source providers.
Strengths
Only public dataset reflecting real production API choices rather than benchmarks or hype. Reveals open-source / Chinese-model adoption invisible elsewhere.
Caveats
No country breakdown. Single-platform: misses direct API users on OpenAI, Anthropic, Google etc. Token volume is a usage signal, not user count.

Cross-source convergent findings

Convergent findings
Finding 01
GDP per capita strongly predicts adoption intensity. Anthropic estimates a 1% rise in GDP/capita is associated with a 0.7% higher AUI. Microsoft documents a ~12.1pp gap between Global North and South.
Finding 02
Small, highly-digitised economies dominate per-capita usage. Israel, Singapore, UAE, Norway, Ireland recur near the top across all three datasets, despite different methodologies.
Finding 03
The US leads in totals, not in intensity. Hosts most frontier models and infrastructure but ranks only 21st (Microsoft Q1 2026) on diffusion. India: #1 by total ChatGPT volume, 76th by per-capita.
Cross-source comparison caveat. These four indicators are not directly commensurable. AUI is a concentration ratio, OpenAI’s rank is per-capita messages, Microsoft’s is population penetration, OpenRouter’s is API token volume by model. The first three speak to consumer adoption; OpenRouter speaks to developer choice. Together they triangulate, but don’t aggregate.

Country panel — Microsoft diffusion by quarter

Selected economies, 2025 H1 – Q1 2026

Microsoft is the only source that publishes per-country time series at quarterly cadence. The chart below tracks twelve representative economies. Diverging trajectories — UAE accelerating, US lagging despite frontier-model leadership — visible in the slopes.

AI diffusion rate (%) by country — quarterly
Microsoft AI Diffusion Reports, 2025 H1 / 2025 H2 / Q1 2026
Fastest movers
South Korea jumped ~5pp in H2 2025 (post-GPT-5 + Ghibli viral moment + Korean-language model improvements). UAE added ~10pp in 9 months. Switzerland +3pp in Q1 2026 alone.
Stagnating leaders
Singapore growth slowing as it approaches saturation (60.9%). Several early-leader European countries (Norway, Ireland) also flattening — suggesting a plateau effect at high diffusion levels.
Catching up
US finally moved from 24th to 21st in Q1 2026 (+3pp). Japan accelerating from a low base. Low-income countries growing at 4× the rate of high-income (OpenAI / NBER, May 2025) — but from much smaller bases.

Why this matters for official statistics
Consumer-facing dashboards (Anthropic, OpenAI, Microsoft) miss a major part of the story: the production AI workloads running through developer APIs increasingly use open-source and Chinese models. Token-level data shifts the picture from a US-led market to a far more fragmented one. For IMF/DGI work on AI in official statistics, this is a measurement gap.

Key trend insight
Inside the US, AI usage is becoming more equal across states — convergence visible in the falling Gini. Across countries, the opposite is happening: concentration is rising. The top 20 countries went from 45% to 48% of population-adjusted usage in six months. Country-level convergence will not happen automatically.
