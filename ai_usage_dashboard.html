Global AI Usage — Three Lenses

A comparative reading of country-level AI adoption indicators from Anthropic, OpenAI, and Microsoft. Same phenomenon, three methodologies, partially overlapping conclusions.

Each company publishes a different indicator. Anthropic measures relative concentration (whether Claude is over- or under-represented in an economy). OpenAI ranks countries by messages-per-capita on consumer plans. Microsoft estimates the absolute share of the working-age population that has used generative AI. The three are not directly comparable — but together they triangulate the global picture.

Anthropic
Economic Index — AUI
What it measures
Ratio of Claude.ai sample share to working-age population share. AUI > 1 = over-represented; < 1 = under-represented.
Data source
Sampled Claude.ai consumer conversations + API usage (Sept '25 release added geographic cuts).
Coverage
150+ countries, all 50 US states + DC.
Strengths
Open-sourced underlying data; intersected with SOC task classifications; tracks augmentation vs. automation.
Caveats
Single-platform measure (Claude.ai only). Concentration metric, not penetration.
OpenAI
Signals — Messages per capita
What it measures
Country rank by messages sent per capita on consumer ChatGPT (Free, Go, Plus, Pro).
Data source
Privacy-preserving sample of messages July 2024 – March 2026, updated quarterly.
Coverage
Countries with population ≥ 5M. Excludes Belarus, China, Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Russia, Venezuela.
Strengths
Largest single-product dataset; topic and demographic decomposition; downloadable time series.
Caveats
Excludes Codex, Enterprise, Education → understates workplace use. Several major markets not covered.
Microsoft
AI Diffusion Report
What it measures
Share of working-age population (15–64) that has used a generative AI product in the period.
Data source
Aggregated, anonymized Microsoft telemetry, adjusted for OS/device share, internet penetration, country population.
Coverage
100+ markets, full population-normalized diffusion rates.
Strengths
Cross-product (captures any genAI use, not only Microsoft's); true penetration metric; quarterly cadence.
Caveats
Telemetry skewed toward Windows/Edge users; adjustment model assumptions are not fully transparent.

What the three sources agree on

Convergent findings
Finding 01
GDP per capita strongly predicts adoption intensity. Anthropic estimates a 1% rise in GDP/capita is associated with a 0.7% higher AUI. Microsoft documents a ~12.1pp gap between Global North and South.
Finding 02
Small, highly-digitized economies dominate per-capita usage. Israel, Singapore, UAE, Norway, Ireland recur near the top across all three datasets, despite different methodologies.
Finding 03
The US leads in totals, not in intensity. Hosts most frontier models and infrastructure but ranks only 21st (Microsoft Q1 '26) on diffusion. India: #1 by total ChatGPT volume, 76th by per-capita.
Cross-source comparison caveat: these three indicators are not directly commensurable. AUI is a concentration ratio, OpenAI's rank is per-capita messages, Microsoft's is population penetration. They agree on rankings and gradients, not on absolute levels.

UAE divergence
Microsoft ranks UAE #1 globally at 70.1%. UAE does not appear at the top tier in Anthropic's AUI. Likely explanation: Microsoft captures Copilot + cross-product telemetry; Anthropic captures Claude.ai sample only.
India paradox
India is #1 by total ChatGPT volume (100M+ WAU) but ranks 76th by per-capita and 0.27× on Anthropic's AUI. Scale and penetration are not the same story.
China gap
China is excluded from OpenAI Signals and underrepresented in Anthropic's data. Microsoft captures some telemetry but DeepSeek now drives most domestic usage — a parallel ecosystem missing from Western dashboards.

Policy implications for statistical institutions

Synthesis
Measurement
No single private-sector indicator is sufficient. Official statistics need a triangulated framework combining vendor telemetry, household ICT surveys, and enterprise adoption surveys — analogous to the multi-source approach already used for digital economy measurement.
Convergence vs. divergence
Anthropic projects US state-level usage could equalize in 2–9 years. Globally, the picture is the opposite: Microsoft's North–South gap is widening. Country-level convergence will not happen by default and requires policy support.
Augmentation vs. automation
Anthropic data shows high-adoption countries lean toward collaborative use, low-adoption countries toward delegated automation. This has direct implications for labor-market projections and skills policy.
Language barrier
Microsoft identifies low-resource language coverage as a persistent constraint. Even after adjusting for GDP and internet access, countries dominated by low-resource languages (Malawi, Laos) show lower adoption.
